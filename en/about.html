About the house

A house born of an observation — not a market opportunity.

Masafa was not founded to answer a commercial gap. It was founded on a precise observation — made by someone who writes poetry and practises engineering — that many Omani texts deserve a place in the Arabic literary scene, and cannot find a house that takes them seriously enough in editing, in design, or in cultural positioning.

Why Masafa exists

The Arabic publishing market suffers a structural split between two poles: commercial houses that measure success by copies sold in the first weeks, and academic presses that address a narrow specialist audience. Between them, the space for a literature that seeks lasting aesthetic and intellectual value shrinks. Masafa exists to occupy and stabilise that middle space.

The problem we came to address

Absence of rigorous editing

Many Arabic publishers treat editing as a step before printing, not an intellectual practice that shapes the text. The result is books that never undergo genuine critical review of their structure, language, or internal coherence.

Erosion of the book as object

The book has become a vessel for text alone — not a material and visual work in its own right. Paper choice, cover design, and page typography are increasingly cost-driven rather than treated as parts of an integrated sensory experience.

The missing Omani place

Omani literature, despite its depth and range, has not yet had an institutional publisher with a clear project to bring it to the Arab reader as it deserves — beyond seasonal festivals and scattered individual efforts.

Vision — three concentric circles

Masafa envisions its future in three circles: Omani first — to be the primary reference the serious Omani writer meets; Gulf next — to bridge to voices sharing its editorial and aesthetic values; Arab beyond — over a horizon greater than a decade, to be named among the independent Arab publishers with a clear project.

Expansion is not an end in itself but a natural consequence of holding steady to our standards. The growth Masafa seeks is growth in standing and influence, not in annual title count.
Philosophy of the Name

Six approaches to the word "Masafa"

The name is not a marketing element chosen after identity work — it is the highest reference from which all subsequent identity decisions branch.

Philosophical

Condition of relation

Distance is not nothingness; it is the condition that makes a relationship between two parties possible. Difference produces meaning, and difference needs a distance to occur in.

Linguistic

Unveiling and journey

From sāfara — to travel — whose root carries the idea of revealing and disclosing. The traveller uncovers the land by crossing it; distance is that trace of unveiling.

Semiotic

Signifier and signified

Every meaning requires a distance between signifier and signified. Literature makes maximal use of this distance — creating an interpretive space for the reader rather than delivering meaning ready-made.

Visual

Whitespace works

Whitespace is not an absence in design but a design element in itself. A page that respects its distances yields a calmer, more respectful reading.

Cultural

Three distances

The distance between writer and reader, the distance between word and its silence, and the distance between Omani literature and the place it deserves in the Arab scene.

Psychological

Reading distance

Serious reading demands a psychological distance from the immediate everyday — a distance of reflection, away from the reactive consumption of much contemporary reading.

Three References

Instruments of measurement — not models to imitate

1

The rigorous editorial model

Gallimard · Faber & Faber. We borrow maximal selectivity, the separation of editorial from marketing in decision-making, and long-term relationships built across an author's successive works.

2

The critical-intellectual model

Galilée. We borrow the courage to publish difficult intellectual texts without simplifying them for market appeal — without falling into complete academic closure to the general reader.

3

The independent Arab model

Dar Al-Adab · Al-Mada · Toubkal. We borrow awareness of the Arab context, the courage to choose titles across national borders, and the discipline of building a trans-regional relationship with the Arab intellectual.

From the Book of Identity · Chapter Eighteen

In the beginning there was no word. There was the distance that precedes the word, and the one that follows it.

At Masafa we do not publish texts; we manage the distances that make texts possible.

From Muscat our texts travel — not to erase the distance between Oman and the Arab world, but to turn that distance into a road to be crossed rather than a barrier to be accepted.
