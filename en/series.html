Series

Six series — unity within variety.

Each series is an independent editorial and visual unit with its own standards, all held under the shared philosophy of the name. Masafa's publications are organised so the book is recognised from the shelf, and every submission is measured against the standards of its series.

ABJAD · SERIES 01

Abjad

Poetry · 3–5 titles/year

The Poetry Series

Reclaiming the poem.

Abjad is the space in which the poem is reclaimed as the highest form of linguistic compression, apart from the fast-cycling waves of digital verse.

  • Single-author collections; anthologies only in exceptional cases.
  • Covers with 60%+ whitespace; no direct photographic imagery.
  • Light editorial intervention with holistic rhythm review across the collection.
  • Omani and Gulf poets first, opening outward from year three.
Fiction & Short Story

A vessel for narrative.

Masrad is the narrative vessel that holds Arabic novels and short stories written with full command of their tools, regardless of length or the author's reputation.

  • Novel, short-story collection, novella.
  • 4–6 titles/year at capacity; the largest of the series.
  • Deeper structural intervention than Abjad.
  • Priority for new narrative voices with unexpended vision.

MIZAN · SERIES 03

Mizan

Criticism · 2–3 titles/year

Literary Criticism

An independent intellectual authority.

Mizan is the reference restoring Arabic literary criticism to its role as an independent authority — not an appendix to literary production.

  • Critical studies, collected essays, adapted theses.
  • More abstract, geometric cover language — indicating the logic of analysis.
  • Terminological review tied to the house glossary.
Translation

A bridge without loss.

Ubour is the bridge across which world literature and thought enter Arabic without losing precision or stylistic voice.

  • Priority for previously untranslated works, or ones that deserve a fresh translation.
  • Dual review: comparison with the source, then a stylistic Arabic pass.
  • Translator's name on the cover at no less than two-thirds the size of the original author's.

LISAN · SERIES 05

Lisan

Linguistics · 1–2 titles/year

Linguistic Studies

Arabic as an object of inquiry.

Lisan is the space where Arabic is treated as an object of rigorous scientific inquiry, not merely an instrument of expression.

  • Arabic linguistics, semantics, comparative grammar, language history.
  • Peer review before final acceptance.
  • Cover language drawn from the phonetic and morphological structure of the letter, not ornamental calligraphy.
Philosophy

Thought produced in Arabic.

Ta'ammul is the space where philosophical thought is produced in Arabic — not only translated or annotated.

  • Original philosophical works in Arabic and comparative studies.
  • Focus on the logical clarity of the argument.
  • The most visually abstract of the series — sharp contrast between whitespace and a single mass of colour.
