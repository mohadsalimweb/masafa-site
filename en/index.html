From the Book of Identity · Chapter Eighteen

Our promise

We do not promise volume. We do not promise speed. We do not promise to please every reader.

We promise one thing only: that every book bearing our name has passed through a sufficient distance of consideration before reaching your hand — a distance of reading, of editing, of design, and a final distance of silence before the press.

We stand between the noise of the market and the silence of the academic archive — not because we fear either, but because we know that taste is not made in the crowd, nor preserved in isolation, but in the middle distance.

Ahmed Al-Shuaili · Founder, Masafa Publishing House · Sultanate of Oman
How We Work

Four principles we do not compromise

01

Editing before printing

Every manuscript passes three reading gates, then three sequential editing stages — structural, stylistic, precise — before design begins.

02

Quality before quantity

Between 4 and 18 titles per year depending on phase. No book is rushed to catch a season; no book is delayed to chase noise.

03

Editorial independence

The editorial decision is free of direct commercial pressure and of funders who would impose an editorial line. Sponsors do not shape content.

04

Whitespace as design

No visual material carries less than 35% whitespace. Visual crowding is a contradiction of the name itself.

Our Ten Values

No department may draft alternative values

1

Quality over volume

Growth in standing, not number of titles.

2

Editing before printing

Editing is intellectual work, not procedure.

3

Thought and beauty together

4

Independence

5

Respect for the author
