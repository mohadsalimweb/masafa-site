Submissions

We read every manuscript. We reply to every manuscript.

Every work sent to the house is logged in the central registry with a reference number and passes through the three reading gates. You will receive a reasoned written response — acceptance or rejection — within the timeframes below.

The Reading Path

Three gates before any publishing contract

1

First read

Up to 21 days. One or two members of the reading committee issue a preliminary acceptance or a reasoned preliminary rejection.

2

Second read

Up to 30 days. Two different committee members, joined by the editor-in-chief. Collective decision: conditional acceptance or final rejection.

3

Final arbitration

Up to 14 days. Chaired by the editor-in-chief, with external advisory input where needed. Outcome: final publishing decision.

What we look for

  • Linguistic command — free of structural errors beyond the boundaries of deliberate stylistic craft.
  • Internal necessity — the shape of the text (length, structure, voice) is justified by the work itself, not arbitrary.
  • Originality of vision — the work offers an angle or voice that does not repeat what has been published in the same series over the last five years.
  • Continuity — the work opens the possibility of a future editorial relationship with the author.
  • Series fit — the work aligns with one of our six series, or founds a new one on justified grounds.

What we do not accept

  • Absence of artistic necessity — texts that could be halved without losing anything essential.
  • Over-reliance on personal biography as a substitute for the text's independent artistic value.
  • Direct imitation of another author's style without transformation or addition.
  • Merely functional use of Fus'ha — grammatically correct prose lacking stylistic density — in literary submissions.
  • Hate speech or direct defamation of individuals outside justified artistic context.
